# Example Questions

Use these as starting points. Replace the details with your real situation.

## Example 1: Should I Quit My Job?

```text
I want to run a Thinkers Roundtable on one decision.

Situation:
I am 32 and working in a stable product role. My monthly take-home income is about $3,500. I have saved about $55,000. For the last six months I have been building a small tool for local businesses. It makes about $850/month in revenue but is not profitable yet. I am considering quitting and working on it full time for one year.

Fear:
The worst case is that the product does not grow, I burn savings, and I return to the job market weaker than before. I may be lying to myself that "full time focus" will solve a product problem that may not be real.

Desired output:
I do not want encouragement. I want to know whether the "full time will make it work" assumption is valid, what I should test before quitting, and what would prove this decision wrong.

Please especially bring in Musk, Taleb, Paul Graham, and Karpathy.
```

## Example 2: Which Project Should I Cut?

```text
I want to run a Thinkers Roundtable on one decision.

Situation:
I am splitting my evenings between a full-time job, a newsletter, and an old software project I want to revive. I only have about three focused hours after work. All three feel important, but none has made clear progress in three months.

Fear:
I may be pretending this is a time-management problem when it is really fear of choosing. Keeping all three alive lets me avoid the pain of being wrong about one.

Desired output:
Help me identify which one to cut, which assumption should decide the priority, and what first test I should run this week.

Please especially bring in Munger, Jobs, Naval, and Paul Graham.
```

## Example 3: Is This AI Workflow Production-Ready?

```text
I want to run a Thinkers Roundtable on one decision.

Situation:
I built an AI workflow that can generate product images from supplier photos. The demo looks good, but I am not sure it is reliable enough for batch use. The main uncertainty is whether it handles messy real-world inputs without manual cleanup.

Fear:
I may be confusing a good demo with a real production system. I may also be spending too much time polishing the workflow before proving that users actually care.

Desired output:
Tell me what to test first, where the failure modes are, and whether the workflow should remain human-in-the-loop.

Please especially bring in Karpathy, Feynman, Jobs, and MrBeast.
```
